Plan the proposal around your partner, not a template

The best proposals are not the biggest ones. They are the ones that feel unmistakably like the two of you. Before I Do helps you slow down and plan around who your partner actually is.

How to use this page: Read it once for context, then use the partner-first questions and Wendy prompts to shape a proposal that fits your relationship, your pace and your city.

Start with your partner's world

Before venues, rings or dates, get clear on the small things that make your partner feel seen: the settings they relax in, the people they trust, and the moments they replay fondly. These details quietly decide whether a proposal lands.

Five partner-first questions

  • Does my partner love a private moment or a shared celebration?
  • What place already holds meaning for us?
  • Who, if anyone, should be in on it?
  • What pace feels right, soon, or a season away?
  • What would feel like too much, and what would feel just right?

Timing without pressure

There is no universal right time, only the right time for your relationship. Use the milestones below as a calm order of operations rather than a countdown.

Understand

Map your partner's pace, love language and the people who matter before deciding anything else.

Imagine

Sketch two or three proposal directions that fit, then pressure-test them against the partner-first questions.

Prepare

Line up the location, any helpers, and a backup plan, keeping logistics light so the moment stays personal.

Carry it forward

After the yes, move straight into the Engagement Planner to turn the moment into a plan.

Keep it local

Your city shapes what is possible, from quiet overlooks to the restaurant where you had your first date. Lean on local context instead of copying ideas built for someone else's town.

Before I Do questions

Quick answers for couples planning the moment before the moment.

What is Before I Do?

Before I Do is the pre-engagement planning lane. It helps you plan a proposal around your partner's personality, pace and love language before any wedding planning begins.

How is this different from the Engagement Planner?

Before I Do happens first, before the yes. The Engagement Planner picks up after the yes and carries the moment into wedding planning, city guides, vendors and a timeline.

Do I need a ring before using Before I Do?

No. Before I Do focuses on intention, timing and your partner's world first. Rings, locations and logistics come after you understand what would feel meaningful to them.
