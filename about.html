About Marriage In My City

A local wedding planning guide from Elite Media Group and the In My City network.

Marriage In My City was built to help couples move from “where do we even start?” to a clear, local plan. We combine researched city guides, practical planning tools, and Wendy — our AI wedding guide — so couples can understand the license path, venue landscape, vendor sequence, guest logistics, registry decisions, and newlywed next steps in one place.

Who we are

Marriage In My City is part of the In My City network, created by Elite Media Group. The network is built around a simple idea: when people are making important local decisions, they should not have to sort through scattered search results, stale directories, generic advice, and ten open tabs just to understand the next step.

Elite Media Group develops local discovery experiences for real-life moments — care, pets, homes, services, weddings, and more — with a focus on helpful guidance, transparent consumer journeys, and city-specific context. Marriage In My City brings that approach to couples planning a wedding and preparing for the life that comes after it.

📍

Local first

Every city has its own rhythm. Miami planning is not Albany planning. Staten Island is not Santa Barbara. We write for the actual place: license offices, seasons, venue styles, travel patterns, family logistics, and the small details that shape the wedding weekend.

✍️

Useful over generic

Our goal is not to publish another pretty checklist. It is to help couples make decisions: what to confirm, what to price, what to book, what to ask, and what to write down before planning gets noisy.

🤖

Wendy is here to help

Wendy is the AI wedding guide built into the site. She helps couples turn ideas, timing, budget, guest count, location, and pressure points into a Bridal Blueprint they can actually use.

🔒

Trust matters

We are transparent about AI, advertising, affiliate relationships, and data practices. Marriage license rules, fees, and office hours can change, so we always encourage couples to confirm official requirements directly with the appropriate clerk or government office.

Meet Wendy

Wendy is the friendly planning layer inside Marriage In My City. Tell her your city, date, guest count, budget range, venue style, family situation, or what feels overwhelming, and she can help organize the next steps.

She is designed to be practical: license reminders, vendor questions, venue considerations, timeline pressure points, registry thinking, and newlywed planning. She does not replace a licensed planner, attorney, clerk, vendor, or your own judgment. She helps you think clearly before you make the call.

You can learn more about how AI is used on this site in our AI Disclosure.

Our editorial approach

Marriage In My City guides are written to feel specific to the community they serve. We cover the same core planning lanes — marriage license path, venue styles, seasonal realities, guest logistics, vendor booking sequence, budget pressure, registry setup, and newlywed planning — but each page should read like it belongs to its city.

When we reference venues, vendor categories, or local planning examples, those mentions are meant to help couples understand the market and the decision in front of them. They should not be treated as endorsements unless clearly labeled as sponsored or partnered placements.

We update content on a rolling basis and welcome corrections. If something looks outdated, incomplete, or off, tell us. Local guidance only works when it stays honest.
